Comprehensive guide to brand naming patterns with 50+ examples across industries.

## 1. Portmanteau (Word Blending)

### How It Works
Combine the beginning of one word with the end of another, or merge words at overlap points.

### Formula Variations
- **Front + Back**: [First word start] + [Second word end]
- **Overlap Merge**: Find common letters/sounds and merge
- **Syllable Splice**: Take syllables from each word

## 2. Phonetic Respelling

### How It Works
Take a common word and spell it uniquely while maintaining pronunciation.

### Techniques
- **Drop vowels**: Remove silent or unnecessary vowels
- **Swap letters**: Use phonetic equivalents (k for c, z for s)
- **Stylize endings**: -er → -r, -le → -l, -tion → -shun

## Quick Reference: Naming by Tone

### Professional/Enterprise
- Use: Tech suffixes (-io, -ify), industry terms
- Avoid: Gaming slang, casual phrases
- Examples: Tradeix, Fundio, AlphaCore

### Friendly/Consumer
- Use: Portmanteaus, phonetic respelling
- Avoid: Complex technical terms
- Examples: Pipular, Tradr, Clevr

### Playful/Youth
- Use: Gaming slang, casual phrases, respelling
- Avoid: Corporate suffixes
- Examples: NoobTrader, LevelUpFunds, Gainz

### Technical/Developer
- Use: -hub, -lab, -base suffixes
- Avoid: Overly casual slang
- Examples: Codehub, Devlab, Apibase
